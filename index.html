MedicalComp. | Home

This is the homepage of our site. Below you will find various graphs displaying information about different cancers and its costs through different phases of care.

We understand that this can be a sensitive topic, if you require assistance with further understanding of what the graphs mean, please contact our helpline where you will reach one of our friendly team members. We hope this information helps and makes you aware of the costs it can have, as well as how different forms can impact this.

MedicalComp | Cancer Cost Treatment Data

Pancreas Cancer

This visualisation talks about pancreas cancer (affecting both male and female as well as all ages). The graphs shows that for different phases of care, there is an increase in cost. Usually, the max amount spent is between £4,000 - £4,500. The year these statistics were taken, were in 2020 so could differ to the current date.

Leukemia Cancer

This visualisation shows the costs for different phases of care for Leukemia cancer. The graph shows that for alternative phases of care, the cost can increase. This is for both genders of all ages. Keep in mind the year these statistics were recorded were in 2015 so it could differ from the current date.

Prostate Cancer

This graph talks about prostate cancer, and how through different phases of care the cost can differ. In particular, this graph shows the statistics for males of all ages. As you can see, the cost can range from £12,990 to £14,200. Again the year these statistics were recorded were in 2014 so could potentially differ from the current date.

Ovary Cancer

This graph talks about ovary cancer and its costs throughout different phases of care. The severity can be a factor and this affects females of all ages. You can see how the different phases affect the cost of treatment. This again is a statistic recorded in a different year so it can differ from the current date.

Cervix Cancer

This visualisation talks about Cervix cancer. This affects females of all ages, and it shows the total cost for treatment throughout different phases of care. As you can see, the cost can range from £1,200 to £1,600. The year this stastic was recorded differs from the current year, so the cost may be different to what it was then.

Oesaughagus Cancer

This visualisation talks about oesaughaus cancer that affects both genders of all ages. The different phases of care can make the cost of the treatment increase, depending what phase the cancer is in. This is a statistic from 2012, so the current treatment cost may differ. Furthermore, the severity of the condition may play a role in deciding the cost.

Breast Cancer

This data visualisation talks about breast cancer that affects females of all ages. As you can see, the treatment cost can differ from whatever phase the cancer is in, however this is a statistic from 2011 so the treatment cost can be different from the current year. Like other forms of cancer, the severity of the cancer can play a major role in cost.

Brain Cancer

This data visualisation talks about the treatment cost for brain cancer that affects all ages and both genders. The graph shows that for different phases of care the range can be different, the worse the phase the more likely you are to pay more. However, this is an old statistic and the cost may differ.

Total Cost for Cancer Sites

Finally, this data visualisation shows the total costs for the different types of cancer sites listed. As you can see, the different forms of cancer can depened on how severe the phase is, as well as the condition. The more risky the treatment is, the more likely there will be an increase in cost. These are stastics from different years, here to give you an idea of what the cost may be like.
